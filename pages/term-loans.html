Business Term Loans

A straightforward capital infusion with a fixed repayment cadence. Predictable monthly obligations from $25K to $500K, maturing over one to five years.

Apply Now →

Structured capital. Absolute clarity.

A term loan delivers a single lump-sum disbursement repaid over a predetermined period in equal installments. There are no variables to track, no floating rates to monitor, and no surprises lurking in the amortization schedule. Every payment is identical from origination to maturity.

This instrument is favored by established businesses pursuing discrete, well-scoped investments — a facility expansion, a strategic inventory purchase, a marketing campaign with defined ROI horizons, or the refinancing of higher-cost legacy obligations.

  • ✓ Principals from $25,000 to $500,000
  • ✓ Unwavering fixed monthly payments
  • ✓ Maturities of 1 to 5 years
  • ✓ Competitive rates reflecting business strength

Where founders deploy term capital.

The best use cases are planned, not reactive.

🏗️

Facility Expansion

Open new locations or extend existing footprints with capital structured around realistic timelines.

🔄

Debt Restructuring

Consolidate multiple high-rate obligations into a single, manageable facility at a superior blended rate.

👥

Team Scaling

Fund the recruitment, onboarding, and initial compensation of key hires before their productivity materializes.

📢

Market Development

Capitalize marketing initiatives with a budget that matches the campaign's planned measurement window.

💻

Technology Investment

Deploy capital against software, hardware, or digital infrastructure with multi-year payoff horizons.

📦

Strategic Procurement

Secure long-term supply contracts or bulk inventory positions at terms unavailable to spot buyers.

Is a term facility the correct structure?

  • ✓ You know exactly how much capital you need and for what purpose
  • ✓ Predictable monthly obligations fit your operating model
  • ✓ Your enterprise has been operating for at least one year
  • ✓ Monthly revenue consistently exceeds $10,000
  • ✓ You value the discipline of a defined amortization schedule
