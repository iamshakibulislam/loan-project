Patient capital. Institutional terms.

SBA loans carry a partial federal guarantee that reduces the lending institution's exposure, enabling them to extend terms that simply aren't available in the conventional market. Rates that begin in the mid-single digits. Maturities measured in decades, not months. Amortization schedules that respect the long arc of business growth.

These facilities are purpose-built for enterprises with established track records that are pursuing substantial, multi-year initiatives — facility acquisition, large-scale equipment deployment, strategic refinancing of legacy debt, or the purchase of another operating business.

Is an SBA facility the right instrument?

SBA programs deliver the best long-term economics available, but the underwriting process is more deliberate than revenue-based or line-of-credit alternatives. The trade-off — patience for price — is often worth it for well-capitalized businesses pursuing initiatives measured in years, not quarters.

  • ✓ Two or more years of operating history with documented financials
  • ✓ Personal credit above 680 — though exceptions exist
  • ✓ Consistent profitability and cash flow coverage
  • ✓ Planning a major capital deployment — expansion, acquisition, or refinance
  • ✓ Seeking the lowest possible cost of capital over a long horizon
