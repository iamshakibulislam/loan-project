About Atlas Fund Capital

What exactly does Atlas Fund Capital do? ▼
We are a private capital advisory firm. We do not lend money directly. We operate as your strategic intermediary — maintaining relationships with a curated consortium of institutional capital providers and matching your business with the sources best positioned to offer competitive terms. Think of us as your capital advisor: one point of contact, access to dozens of funding sources, and a team that negotiates on your behalf.
How much capital can I access through Atlas? ▼
Our consortium can accommodate facilities ranging from $5,000 to $20 million. The specific amount you qualify for depends on your revenue profile, operating history, credit standing, and the type of financing that best fits your use case. The qualification form on our homepage gives you a preliminary indication within ninety seconds.
Is my information secure? ▼
Yes. We employ enterprise-grade encryption across all touchpoints and never share your personal or business data with any capital source until you've reviewed and expressed interest in their terms. Our initial credit assessment uses a soft inquiry that leaves no trace on your report. Your data is never sold, monetized, or shared beyond the specific purpose of identifying suitable funding options.

The Process

Will this inquiry show up on my credit report? ▼
No. Our initial qualification uses a soft credit pull — the kind that's visible only to you and has zero impact on your score. A formal inquiry occurs only if you accept a specific financing offer and authorize the institution to proceed with full underwriting. You can explore every option available to you without leaving a single footprint on your credit profile.
How long does the entire process take? ▼
The qualification form takes roughly ninety seconds. A senior advisor typically reviews your profile and presents initial options within hours. If you accept an offer, revenue-based facilities and lines of credit can fund the same day. Term products generally settle in 24 to 72 hours. SBA-backed programs follow a more deliberate timeline — two to four weeks — but deliver the most favorable long-term terms.
What information do I need to provide? ▼
To begin: just what's on the qualification form — the amount you need, your monthly revenue, approximate credit standing, and how long you've been in business. No tax returns, no bank statements, no documentation required to receive initial options. If you choose to proceed with a specific offer, the funding institution will request standard verification materials — typically 3 to 6 months of business bank statements and identification.

Eligibility

What are the minimum requirements? ▼
Each institution in our consortium maintains its own criteria, but the typical baseline includes: at least 6 months of operating history, monthly revenue exceeding $10,000, and a personal credit score above 500. If your profile falls outside these parameters, your advisor can often identify alternative paths — particularly for businesses with strong recent revenue growth or secured contracts.
My credit isn't where I'd like it. Are there options? ▼
Absolutely. Several of our capital sources specialize in working with founders whose credit profiles are still developing. For certain products — particularly revenue-based financing — your business's actual cash flow matters substantially more than your personal FICO. Rates will reflect the risk profile, but doors remain open for scores as low as 500.
Do I need to put up collateral? ▼
Many of our facilities are unsecured — particularly revenue-based products, lines of credit, and shorter-term instruments. Most institutions require a personal guarantee from the business owner, which is standard in commercial lending. SBA loans and larger facilities may require specific collateral. Equipment financing uses the equipment itself as security. Your advisor will walk through any collateral requirements transparently before you commit.
What if my business is brand new? ▼
Enterprises with less than six months of operating history face a narrower set of options. However, strong personal credit (680+), demonstrable industry experience, or signed client contracts can open doors that would otherwise remain closed. SBA microloans and certain credit products are sometimes available to newer ventures. Your advisor can assess your specific situation.

Costs

Is there a fee to apply or get qualified? ▼
No. There is no cost to apply, to receive options, or to consult with your advisor. We are compensated by the funding institution when — and only when — you accept an offer and close. You will never receive an invoice from Atlas Fund Capital. The terms you see are the terms you get.
What kind of rates should I expect? ▼
Rates are a function of your specific profile and the product type. General benchmarks: SBA programs range from 6% to 10% APR. Conventional term facilities range from 7% to 30% APR depending on the institution and your qualifications. Lines of credit typically span 8% to 24%. Revenue-based products use factor rates rather than APR — your advisor will translate every offer into its true annualized cost before you decide.
Are there prepayment penalties? ▼
This varies by institution and product. Most term facilities and lines of credit carry no prepayment penalty — you can retire the obligation early and save on interest. Revenue-based products typically have a fixed total cost regardless of prepayment timing. Your advisor will flag any prepayment provisions in plain language before you accept any offer.
